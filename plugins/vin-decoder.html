Decode VINs & read them phonetically over the phone

VINs use 17 characters (A-Z, 0-9 — no I, O, or Q). Press Enter to decode.

📖 VIN Structure Reference ▼
PositionSectionWhat It Means
1 WMI Country where the vehicle was manufactured
2 WMI Manufacturer (e.g. Ford, Toyota, BMW)
3 WMI Vehicle type or manufacturing division
4–8 VDS Body style, engine type, restraint system, transmission
9 VDS Check digit — validates the VIN mathematically
10 VIS Model year (A=2010, B=2011... S=2025, T=2026)
11 VIS Assembly plant code
12–17 VIS Sequential production / serial number
WMI = World Manufacturer Identifier  ·  VDS = Vehicle Descriptor Section  ·  VIS = Vehicle Identifier Section

APCO Phonetic Alphabet

A — AdamB — BoyC — Charles D — DavidE — EdwardF — Frank G — GeorgeH — HenryJ — John K — KingL — LincolnM — Mary N — NoraP — PaulR — Robert S — SamT — TomU — Union V — VictorW — WilliamX — X-ray Y — YoungZ — Zebra

Letters I, O, and Q are never used in VINs to avoid confusion with 1, 0, and 9.
